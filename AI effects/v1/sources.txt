citekey:    shumailov2024ai
authors:    Ilia Shumailov et al. (Oxford / Cambridge / Toronto)
year:       2024
title:      AI Models Collapse When Trained on Recursively Generated Data
type:       Peer-reviewed journal article (Nature, vol. 631)
url:        https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-07566-y
doi:        10.1038/s41586-024-07566-y
themes:     9 (synthetic content contamination)
status:     candidate
pdf:        yes
access:     paywall-user (Nature — user to download)
copyright:  Nature (all rights reserved)
summary:    Foundational empirical paper establishing model collapse as real and measurable.
            Training successive model generations on their own outputs causes irreversible
            degradation — tails of the original data distribution progressively disappear.
            Demonstrated across language models, VAEs, and Gaussian mixtures. Ninth-generation
            model trained recursively on a medieval architecture passage produced only jackrabbit
            lists. Indiscriminate scraping of AI-generated web content poses structural risk to
            model quality.
notes:      Gold standard source for model collapse. Published in Nature. A qualifying response
            exists (arXiv:2410.12954) but does not refute the core finding.

citekey:    gerstgrasser2024model
authors:    Matthias Gerstgrasser et al. (Stanford / SALT Lab)
year:       2024
title:      Is Model Collapse Inevitable? Breaking the Curse of Recursion by Accumulating Real and Synthetic Data
type:       Preprint (arXiv:2404.01413); under review NeurIPS 2024
url:        https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.01413
doi:        —
themes:     9 (synthetic content contamination — qualifying/contradicting)
status:     candidate
pdf:        no
access:     open
copyright:  arXiv preprint (open access)
summary:    Directly challenges inevitability of model collapse. Distinguishes "replace" regime
            (synthetic replaces prior data → collapse) from "accumulate" regime (synthetic added
            on top of all prior real data → no collapse). Argues data pipeline design, not
            AI-generated content per se, is the critical variable. Main peer-reviewed
            counterargument to Shumailov et al.
notes:      Key counterevidence — factual qualification, not interpretive disagreement.
            The "accumulate" condition requires stable access to clean historical data,
            which degrades as the web fills with AI content. Cite alongside shumailov2024ai.
